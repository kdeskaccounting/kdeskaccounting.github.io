If your lease amortization schedule re-builds itself every time someone changes a payment, it's not a schedule. It's a guess.

The pattern I see in finance teams that get ASC 842 right: they separate three things that most spreadsheets confuse.

1. The **lease liability schedule** — payment, interest, principal, ending balance. Pure math.
2. The **ROU asset schedule** — for operating leases this is the residual; for finance leases it's straight-line depreciation. Different rules.
3. The **expense recognition schedule** — operating leases produce a single straight-line expense; finance leases produce interest + depreciation that's front-loaded.

A spreadsheet that mixes these tabs is one rate-change away from breaking. A spreadsheet that keeps them separate ties out every period regardless of inputs.
