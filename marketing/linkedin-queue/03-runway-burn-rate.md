The runway number your board asks for and the runway number your team builds are usually different. That gap is where startups die.

Here's where the calculations usually diverge:

- **Net burn vs gross burn** — most teams report gross. Boards want net. The difference is whether the company is funded by revenue or by the bank account.
- **Recurring vs one-time burn** — a quarterly software contract renewal isn't burn, it's deferred OPEX. But if you forget to model it, it shows up like burn and crushes the runway number.
- **AP timing** — runway calculated on cash balance ignores the $400K in invoices sitting unpaid. Real runway is cash + AR − AP, not just cash.

Most importantly: runway is not a single number. It's a month-by-month cash bridge. A "12-month runway" averaged across the year often hides a 6-month cliff in month 4.
