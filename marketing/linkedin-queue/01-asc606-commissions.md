The fastest way to fail an ASC 606 commission audit isn't a formula error. It's amortizing over the wrong period.

I see this every quarter. Finance teams capitalize the commission, set up a 12-month amortization, and move on. Then the auditor asks the question every CFO has had to answer: "Why 12 months when your customers renew on average 3.4 times?"

ASC 340-40 lets you pick between contract term and estimated benefit period. Both are acceptable. The choice has to be deliberate and documented — not a default.

Three rules I apply:

1. If contracts are ≤12 months, immediately expense unless renewals are highly likely
2. If renewals materially extend the relationship, estimated benefit period is usually right — and Big 4 auditors prefer it for SaaS
3. Pick the method, document it, apply it consistently. Switching methods to flatter quarterly results is what triggers the audit drag.
